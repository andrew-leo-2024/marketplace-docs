---

## What This Track Produces

Graduates can deploy AI compliance systems across regulated industries, translate regulatory requirements into machine-executable constraints, operate compliance monitoring platforms, and manage the intersection of AI deployment and regulatory obligation. They bridge the gap between legal text and system behavior.

---

## Curriculum Overview

### Week 1: Regulatory Landscape for AI Deployment
- Global AI regulation mapping: EU AI Act (risk tiers, prohibited practices, transparency obligations), US framework (state-level patchwork, sector-specific rules), APAC regulations (Singapore AIDA, Japan, Korea, Australia)
- Sector-specific compliance: financial services (AML/KYC, consumer protection), healthcare (HIPAA, clinical decision support), government (procurement rules, data sovereignty)
- Regulatory trajectory: where regulations are heading, what will be required in 12--24 months

### Week 2: Translating Regulation to Machine-Executable Constraints
- Converting legal requirements into system specifications: what "transparency" means technically, what "human oversight" requires architecturally
- Designing compliance-by-default: building regulatory adherence into system architecture, not bolting it on
- The MCO (Mortality Compliance Object) framework: encoding time-bound compliance obligations into system behavior

### Week 3: Compliance Operations
- Operating compliance monitoring platforms: alert management, false positive handling, escalation workflows
- Compliance evidence production: generating audit-ready documentation from system logs
- Cross-jurisdictional compliance management: satisfying conflicting requirements simultaneously

### Week 4: Capstone Project
- Design a compliance deployment plan for a simulated regulated AI system
- Include regulatory mapping, constraint specifications, monitoring architecture, and evidence production workflows
- Present with regulatory adversarial review (instructor plays the regulator)
