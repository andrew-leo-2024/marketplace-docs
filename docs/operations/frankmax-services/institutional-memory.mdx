## What It Does

Institutional Memory & Accountability Continuity preserves decision context, rationale, authority chains, and governance history beyond individual leadership tenure. It solves the problem that every organization faces: when the person who made a critical decision leaves, the reason they made it leaves with them.

This is not a knowledge management system. It is an **accountability continuity platform** -- designed specifically to ensure that:

1. **Decision rationale survives leadership transitions** -- Why a decision was made, what alternatives were considered, what constraints applied
2. **Authority chains remain traceable** -- Who had authority when, how it was delegated, when it was revoked
3. **Governance history compounds** -- Each year of operation adds to the defensibility record rather than starting fresh
4. **Regulatory responses remain possible** -- When a regulator asks about a decision made three years ago by a person who left two years ago, the organization can still produce a complete answer

---

## Why This Matters

The average tenure of a C-suite executive is 4.9 years. The average regulatory investigation looks back 5--7 years. This gap is structural: the people who made the decisions being investigated are gone. Their context, rationale, and informal knowledge went with them.

Organizations without institutional memory systems face a choice during investigation: reconstruct context from incomplete records (expensive, slow, unreliable) or admit they cannot explain their own decisions (catastrophic for defensibility).

## Service Components

### Platform Deployment
- Deploy the institutional memory capture layer integrated with existing decision workflows
- Configure automated context capture (decision rationale, alternatives considered, constraints applied, evidence reviewed)
- Establish authority chain versioning (who had what authority, when, under what conditions)

### Knowledge Architecture Advisory
- Design the taxonomy for captured institutional knowledge (decision categories, authority types, governance domains)
- Define retention policies aligned with regulatory requirements across applicable jurisdictions
- Establish access controls and privilege structures for sensitive governance history

### Ongoing Advisory
- Monthly review of capture completeness metrics
- Quarterly assessment of knowledge architecture adequacy as the organization evolves
- Annual stress-test: simulate a regulatory inquiry requiring historical decision reconstruction
