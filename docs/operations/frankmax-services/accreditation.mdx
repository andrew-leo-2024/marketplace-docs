## What It Does

Accreditation & External Defensibility Signals provides third-party certification that an organization's governance structures meet defensibility standards. It is the external proof that internal governance is real -- not theater.

The accreditation serves three functions:

1. **Regulatory signal** -- Demonstrates to regulators that the organization has invested in governance proactively, not reactively
2. **Insurance signal** -- Provides insurers with verified governance data to support favorable AI liability coverage terms
3. **Procurement signal** -- Satisfies enterprise and government procurement requirements for vendor governance certification

---

## Why This Matters

Internal governance has a credibility problem. Every organization claims to have "robust governance frameworks." No one believes them until there is independent verification.

Accreditation converts internal governance investment into externally verifiable claims. It is the difference between "we have a risk management program" and "our risk management program has been independently assessed and certified."

As AI liability insurance becomes standard, insurers will require governance verification before underwriting. Organizations with accreditation will receive coverage; organizations without it will face exclusions or premium surcharges.

## Certification Process

### Initial Assessment (2--4 weeks)
- Review existing governance structures (authority maps, decision frameworks, evidence architecture, institutional memory)
- Assess against the Frankmax Defensibility Standard (covering authority clarity, evidence completeness, containment readiness, and accountability continuity)
- Identify gaps requiring remediation before certification

### Gap Remediation (if required)
- Provide specific remediation guidance for identified gaps
- Re-assess after remediation is complete
- Partial certifications available for organizations in progress

### Certification
- Issue accreditation certificate with scope, date, and validity period
- Publish compliance report detailing assessed domains and scores
- Register the certification in the Frankmax Accreditation Registry

### Annual Renewal
- Annual re-assessment against updated standards
- Review of governance changes since last certification
- Updated certificate and compliance report
