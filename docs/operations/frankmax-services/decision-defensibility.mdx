## What It Does

Decision Defensibility Structuring re-engineers approval processes so that every consequential decision is defensible under oath. It does not create compliance theater -- it builds the structural capacity to prove, in adversarial proceedings, that a decision was:

1. **Authorized** -- Made by someone with documented authority to make it
2. **Informed** -- Based on evidence that was available and reviewed at the time
3. **Constrained** -- Subject to appropriate limits, thresholds, and escalation triggers
4. **Traceable** -- Recorded with sufficient context to reconstruct the decision rationale years later
5. **Supervised** -- Subject to human oversight where required by regulation or policy

The output is not a policy document. It is a **decision architecture** -- a set of structured processes, evidence templates, and system configurations that produce defensibility as a byproduct of normal operations.

---

## Why Ongoing Advisory

Defensibility is not a one-time fix. Decision architectures degrade as:

- New AI systems are deployed without governance integration
- Personnel changes break authority chains
- Regulatory requirements shift across jurisdictions
- Organizational restructuring creates new decision nodes without defensibility coverage

The ongoing retainer ensures that the decision architecture evolves with the organization rather than decaying into irrelevance.

## Service Components

### Decision Architecture Design
- Map every consequential decision category (AI deployment, model selection, data usage, automated actions)
- Design approval workflows with embedded evidence capture
- Define escalation triggers and override conditions with documentation requirements

### Evidence Package Templates
- Create standardized evidence packages for each decision category
- Define what constitutes sufficient evidence for defensibility (not "what we capture" but "what a court would require")
- Design evidence retention policies aligned with jurisdictional requirements

### System Configuration
- Configure existing tools (approval workflows, document management, audit logging) to produce defensibility evidence automatically
- Identify gaps where manual evidence capture is required and design lightweight capture processes
- Integrate with existing GRC (Governance, Risk, Compliance) platforms

### Ongoing Monitoring & Tuning
- Monthly review of decision defensibility metrics (evidence completeness, authority chain integrity, constraint compliance)
- Quarterly assessment of new AI deployments and their governance integration
- Annual defensibility stress-test simulating adversarial review
