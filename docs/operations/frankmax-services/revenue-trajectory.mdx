## Revenue Model Mechanics

### Phase 1: Cash Generation (Months 1--3)

PIAR is the entry point. Every engagement is a diagnostic that reveals structural gaps requiring remediation. The conversion path:

1. **PIAR engagement** ($15K--$75K) identifies 3--5 critical accountability gaps
2. **Authority & Liability Mapping** ($10K--$50K) resolves the "who authorized this?" gap
3. Combined revenue per client: $25K--$125K in the first engagement cycle

At 1--2 engagements per month, this generates $25K--$250K in the first quarter with zero infrastructure costs.

### Phase 2: Depth Expansion (Months 4--6)

Clients who completed PIAR and Authority Mapping have documented evidence of deeper structural risks. The natural next step:

1. **Failure Propagation Analysis** ($20K--$100K) models how identified gaps cascade
2. **Jurisdictional Exposure** ($25K--$150K) maps cross-border enforcement risk

These are larger engagements sold to board-level buyers based on PIAR findings.

### Phase 3: Recurring Revenue (Months 7--12)

The diagnostic and structural services create demand for ongoing advisory:

1. **Decision Defensibility Structuring** ($5K--$20K/month) -- ongoing retainer
2. **Institutional Memory** ($3K--$10K/month) -- platform + advisory retainer

Each client converting to monthly retainers generates $8K--$30K/month in recurring revenue. Five clients on retainer = $40K--$150K/month.

### Phase 4: Certification Revenue (Year 2+)

**Accreditation** ($10K--$50K/year per client) creates an annual renewal cycle:

- Every client who completed structural services is a candidate for certification
- Certification creates procurement requirements that drive new client acquisition
- Annual renewal provides predictable, recurring revenue

## The Inevitability Argument

Three structural forces make this revenue trajectory increasingly inevitable over time:

### 1. Structurally Empty Market
No established consulting firm specializes in pre-incident AI accountability. The Big Four offer post-incident forensics. Law firms offer litigation defense. No one offers the structured, pre-incident defensibility service that PIAR represents. This gap will not last forever, but the first mover captures the methodology standard.

### 2. One-Way Adoption
Organizations that complete a PIAR do not reverse course. They do not decide they prefer unexamined accountability exposure. Every engagement deepens the relationship and creates demand for the next service. Churn on structural governance services is near zero because the alternative is willful negligence.

### 3. Insurance Integration
As AI liability insurance matures, insurers will require governance verification as a condition of coverage. Frankmax Accreditation becomes the proof-of-governance that insurers demand. This converts the certification from "nice to have" to "condition of doing business" -- the same structural shift that made SOC 2 mandatory for SaaS vendors.
