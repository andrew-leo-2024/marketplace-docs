## What It Does

Jurisdictional Exposure & Enforcement Readiness maps cross-border legal exposure for organizations deploying AI systems across multiple regulatory regimes. It answers three questions:

1. **Which jurisdictions can enforce what?** -- Mapping the specific regulatory powers, penalties, and enforcement mechanisms in each jurisdiction where the organization operates or processes data.
2. **Against whom?** -- Identifying which legal entities, officers, and individuals within the organization face personal or corporate liability in each jurisdiction.
3. **How prepared is the organization to respond?** -- Assessing whether the organization can satisfy enforcement demands (document production, testimony, data localization) within required timelines.

---

## Why This Matters

AI systems do not respect jurisdictional boundaries. A model trained on data from one jurisdiction, deployed in another, and serving customers in a third creates regulatory exposure in all three. The EU AI Act, US state-level AI laws, Singapore's AIDA framework, and emerging regulations in the GCC, ASEAN, and AU all impose different obligations with different enforcement mechanisms.

Organizations that map their compliance to a single jurisdiction discover -- during enforcement -- that they are exposed in five others. This service eliminates that discovery-under-fire.

## Engagement Structure

### Phase 1: Jurisdictional Inventory (5--7 days)
- Map every jurisdiction where the organization operates, processes data, serves customers, or hosts infrastructure
- Inventory applicable regulations in each jurisdiction (AI-specific, data protection, sector-specific, consumer protection)
- Identify regulatory bodies with enforcement authority and their track records

### Phase 2: Exposure Mapping (5--10 days)
- For each jurisdiction, map specific obligations: notification requirements, data localization, algorithmic transparency, human oversight mandates
- Identify conflicts between jurisdictions (obligations in one jurisdiction that violate requirements in another)
- Map personal liability exposure for officers, directors, and named decision-makers

### Phase 3: Enforcement Scenario Modeling (5--7 days)
- Model 5--10 enforcement scenarios: regulatory investigation, data subject complaint, cross-border warrant, parliamentary inquiry
- For each scenario, trace the enforcement path: which authority initiates, what they demand, what timelines apply, what penalties attach
- Assess organizational readiness to respond to each scenario within required timelines

### Phase 4: Deliverable Production (3--5 days)
- Produce the Jurisdictional Exposure Report with visual maps and enforcement scenario models
- Score each jurisdiction on exposure severity, enforcement probability, and organizational readiness
- Deliver a remediation roadmap prioritized by exposure severity
