## What It Does

Authority & Liability Mapping traces every decision authority in the organization. It answers three questions for every decision that matters:

1. **Who can authorize this?** -- Named individuals, roles, and conditions under which authority is granted.
2. **Under what constraints?** -- Dollar thresholds, approval chains, escalation triggers, override conditions.
3. **Who inherits the liability?** -- When the decision produces consequences, who is accountable -- legally, financially, and reputationally?

Most organizations cannot answer these questions for their AI deployments. Authority is assumed, not documented. Liability is diffuse, not mapped. Constraints exist in policy documents that no one reads and no system enforces.

This service produces a machine-readable, auditable map that connects every decision node to its authority source and liability destination.

---

## Why This Matters

Regulators and courts do not ask "what is your AI governance policy?" They ask "who authorized this specific decision, under what authority, and who is liable for the outcome?" If the organization cannot produce a clear, documented answer in real-time, the question becomes evidence of negligence.

Authority & Liability Mapping eliminates the gap between organizational structure and legal defensibility.

## Engagement Structure

### Phase 1: Authority Discovery (3--5 days)
- Interview decision-makers across business units, legal, risk, and IT
- Map formal authority structures (board resolutions, delegation matrices, signing authorities)
- Identify informal authority patterns (who actually decides, regardless of policy)

### Phase 2: Liability Tracing (3--5 days)
- For each authority node, trace the liability chain: who bears legal, financial, and regulatory consequences
- Identify liability gaps (decisions where no one is clearly accountable)
- Map liability inheritance patterns (when a decision-maker leaves, who inherits their exposure)

### Phase 3: Constraint Mapping (3--5 days)
- Document the conditions under which each authority can be exercised
- Identify missing constraints (authorities without limits) and orphaned constraints (limits without enforcement)
- Map escalation paths and override conditions

### Phase 4: Deliverable Production (2--3 days)
- Produce the Authority & Liability Map as a visual diagram with supporting documentation
- Score each authority node on clarity (documented vs. assumed), enforceability (system-enforced vs. policy-only), and liability coverage (clear vs. diffuse)
- Deliver prioritized recommendations for gap remediation
