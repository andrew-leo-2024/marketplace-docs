# Trust-as-a-Service (Byproduct)

## What It Is

Trust-as-a-Service as a byproduct is distinct from the TaaS service layer. While the service layer issues trust credentials for individual AI outputs, the byproduct is the aggregate trust dataset that emerges from millions of trust credential issuances, verifications, and revocations across the platform. This dataset constitutes a trust graph: a map of who trusts whom, for what, under what conditions, and with what verification frequency in the AI economy.

This trust graph answers questions that no existing dataset can: "Which organizations have the highest AI output trustworthiness scores?" "Which model providers produce outputs that recipients verify most frequently (indicating trust deficit)?" "How does trust propagate through supply chains -- does trusting one organization's AI outputs lead to trusting their partners' outputs?" The trust graph is the reputation infrastructure of the AI economy, and it emerges naturally from platform operations.

## How It's Generated

The TaaS service layer generates trust data from:

- Credential issuance patterns: which organizations issue the most trust credentials, for what types of outputs
- Verification frequency: how often recipients verify credentials (high verification = low baseline trust)
- Revocation events: when and why trust credentials are revoked (trust breakdown indicators)
- Trust chains: how trust propagates from one organization to partners and suppliers
- Cross-sector trust patterns: which industries have the highest and lowest AI output trust levels
- Trust evolution: how organizational trust scores change over time based on credential performance

## Compounding Effect

The trust graph compounds through network effects. Each new organization that joins the platform adds nodes and edges to the trust graph. Each trust credential issued or verified adds signal. Each revocation adds negative signal. Over time, the trust graph becomes comprehensive enough to serve as the credit bureau equivalent for AI trustworthiness. By year three, the question "What is their FrankMax trust score?" becomes standard due diligence. This cannot be replicated without building an equivalent trust credential network, which requires an equivalent installed base of organizations issuing and verifying credentials.
