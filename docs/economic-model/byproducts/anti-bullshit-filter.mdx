# Anti-Bullshit Filter

## What It Is

The Anti-Bullshit Filter is the byproduct of tracking the gap between what AI vendors claim their models can do and what those models actually do in production across thousands of deployments. Every AI provider publishes benchmark scores, accuracy claims, and capability descriptions. The FrankMax platform observes actual performance across real business tasks, real data, and real organizational contexts. The delta between claims and reality -- measured empirically across the customer base -- constitutes the Anti-Bullshit Filter.

This filter exists because the AI industry has a credibility problem. Model providers claim 95% accuracy on benchmarks that do not represent production conditions. AI consultants promise transformational outcomes without evidence. Vendors pitch capabilities that degrade within weeks of deployment. The Anti-Bullshit Filter cuts through this by providing empirical, production-validated performance data. It tells buyers: "Here is what this model actually does in your industry, on your type of data, at your scale -- not what the provider's marketing deck says."

## How It's Generated

The filter builds from the gap between promised and observed performance:

- Benchmark vs. production accuracy: provider-claimed accuracy vs. actual accuracy on real business tasks
- Capability vs. reality: vendor-claimed capabilities vs. actual capability boundaries observed in deployment
- Durability: how long model performance holds after deployment (many degrade within 90 days)
- Edge case frequency: how often models encounter inputs outside their effective range in production
- Cost reality: actual compute cost vs. provider pricing models (hidden costs, overages, inefficiencies)
- Integration friction: actual deployment complexity vs. vendor-promised ease of integration

## Compounding Effect

The Anti-Bullshit Filter gains authority with scale and time. At 100 deployments per provider, it provides directional accuracy data. At 1,000 deployments per provider, it provides statistically rigorous performance benchmarks. At 10,000 deployments, it becomes the definitive independent source of AI model performance data -- the "Consumer Reports" of AI. This authority compounds because each new production deployment adds to the dataset, and the dataset's credibility grows with its breadth. By year three, AI procurement without consulting the Anti-Bullshit Filter becomes a due diligence failure.
