# Human Accountability Infrastructure

## What It Is

Human Accountability Infrastructure is the byproduct of the ETLB (Execution-Time Liability Binding) protocol operating across every AI action on the platform. Every time an AI system makes a decision, processes a document, or generates an output, ETLB binds that action to a named human with defined authority. Over thousands of organizations and millions of AI actions, this creates the largest dataset of human-AI accountability relationships in existence.

This dataset reveals how organizations actually assign responsibility for AI decisions -- not how their policies say they do it, but how they actually do it in practice. It shows which roles approve which types of AI actions, which authority levels are used for which risk categories, which humans are over-assigned (accountability bottlenecks), and which AI actions have no meaningful human oversight despite policy claims to the contrary. This operational truth about human accountability is invaluable to regulators, insurers, and boards.

## How It's Generated

The ETLB protocol generates continuous accountability data:

- Binding events: who approved what AI action, at what authority level, at what time
- Escalation patterns: how often and why AI actions are escalated to higher authority
- Approval latency: how long humans take to review and approve AI decisions
- Override frequency: how often humans override AI recommendations and with what outcomes
- Accountability concentration: how many AI actions are bound to a single human (bottleneck risk)
- Cross-organizational patterns: how similar organizations structure accountability differently

## Compounding Effect

Accountability data compounds as organizations mature in their AI usage. Early data shows initial accountability structures. Over 12-24 months, the data reveals how those structures evolve under pressure: which assignments hold, which break, and which are gamed. By year three, the platform has a longitudinal dataset showing the full lifecycle of human-AI accountability across industries. This longitudinal data is what regulators need to write evidence-based AI governance rules, and no other source can provide it.
