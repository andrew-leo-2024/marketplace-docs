# Time-Indexed Knowledge Markets

## What It Is

Time-Indexed Knowledge Markets is the byproduct of the platform logging every AI-generated insight, analysis, and decision with a precise timestamp and organizational context. Over time, this creates a temporal knowledge graph: what was known, by whom, at what time, and what decisions were made based on that knowledge. This is not a static knowledge base -- it is a time-series database of organizational intelligence that reveals how knowledge evolves, propagates, and decays.

The temporal dimension is what makes this byproduct unique. Traditional knowledge management captures what an organization knows today. Time-Indexed Knowledge Markets capture what the organization knew on any given date, how that knowledge changed over time, and the lag between when new information was available and when it was acted upon. This temporal gap -- the latency between knowing and doing -- is where most organizational value is lost, and it can only be measured by a system that timestamps everything.

## How It's Generated

The platform's logging infrastructure generates temporal knowledge data from:

- AI-generated insights with timestamps and organizational context
- Decision timing: when insights were generated vs. when they were acted upon
- Knowledge propagation: how quickly insights spread across departments and organizations
- Knowledge decay: how long insights remain valid before becoming outdated
- Cross-organization knowledge timing: which organizations learn things first and which lag
- Seasonal and cyclical patterns: how knowledge generation and utilization patterns repeat

## Compounding Effect

Time-Indexed Knowledge Markets become more valuable with every passing day because the time series gets longer. Year one provides 12 months of temporal data. Year three provides longitudinal patterns spanning economic cycles, regulatory changes, and technology transitions. By year five, the platform has the most comprehensive temporal knowledge graph in existence, capable of answering questions like: "How long does it typically take for a regulatory change to propagate through an industry's AI operations?" This temporal depth is impossible to replicate because it requires years of continuous operation that a new entrant cannot compress.
