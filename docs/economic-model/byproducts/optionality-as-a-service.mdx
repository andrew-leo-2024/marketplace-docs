# Optionality-as-a-Service

## What It Is

Optionality-as-a-Service is the byproduct of tracking every decision path not taken across the platform. When an organization uses the marketplace to evaluate three AI models and deploys one, the platform retains performance data on all three. When a workflow branches at a decision point, the platform logs the road not taken. Over time, this creates a massive library of counterfactual data: what would have happened if different choices were made.

This counterfactual library becomes saleable optionality. Organizations can query the platform to answer: "What would our compliance posture look like if we had chosen Model B instead of Model A?" or "What would the cost impact be if we switched from Provider X to Provider Y?" This data is impossible to generate through traditional means because it requires running parallel experiments at scale. The platform generates it as a byproduct of normal operations.

## How It's Generated

Every model selection, workflow configuration, and service layer attachment creates a decision tree with observable and counterfactual branches. The platform logs:

- Model performance comparisons during selection (all evaluated models, not just the chosen one)
- Workflow path analysis at every decision branch
- Pricing sensitivity data from bundle selections and upgrade decisions
- Service layer attachment sequences and the outcomes of different attachment orderings
- Cross-customer aggregated data on which choices correlated with which outcomes

## Compounding Effect

Optionality data compounds exponentially. With 100 customers making 10 decisions each, the platform has 1,000 decision paths. With 10,000 customers, it has 100,000 decision paths with cross-correlation data. At scale, the platform can predict the outcome of decisions that no individual customer has ever made by interpolating from the aggregate dataset. This predictive optionality is unsellable on day one but becomes the most valuable strategic planning dataset in AI by year three.
