# Legibility-as-Infrastructure

## What It Is

Legibility-as-Infrastructure is the byproduct of the platform making every AI operation readable, understandable, and interpretable to non-technical stakeholders. Every audit trail, every governance report, every compliance document, and every decision explanation generated on the platform contributes to a growing infrastructure of AI legibility -- standardized formats, vocabularies, and frameworks for describing what AI systems do in language that lawyers, regulators, board members, and citizens can understand.

This legibility infrastructure solves one of the fundamental barriers to AI adoption: the inability of decision-makers to understand what AI systems are actually doing. A board member cannot oversee what they cannot read. A regulator cannot regulate what they cannot parse. A citizen cannot trust what they cannot comprehend. The platform generates legibility as a byproduct of governance operations, and over time this legibility infrastructure becomes a standard that other platforms must adopt to remain compatible with regulators and oversight bodies.

## How It's Generated

Platform operations generate legibility artifacts from:

- Decision explanations: human-readable descriptions of why AI systems made specific decisions
- Governance narratives: plain-language descriptions of AI governance structures and policies
- Audit translations: conversion of technical audit logs into narratives understandable by non-technical auditors
- Risk descriptions: standardized frameworks for describing AI risk in business terms
- Compliance summaries: executive-readable compliance status reports
- Cross-organization standardization: common vocabulary emerging from thousands of organizations describing similar AI operations

## Target Buyers

- Board governance committees needing to understand AI operations
- Regulators requiring standardized AI documentation formats
- Legal teams translating technical AI evidence for litigation
- Compliance teams producing regulator-readable AI assessments
- Industry standards bodies adopting common AI description frameworks
- Journalism and public accountability organizations investigating AI systems

## Compounding Effect

Legibility infrastructure compounds through standardization effects. As more organizations use the platform's legibility frameworks, those frameworks become de facto standards. Regulators reference them in guidance documents. Auditors expect them in audit evidence. Courts accept them as evidence formats. This standardization creates a network effect: the more widely adopted the legibility standards become, the more valuable the platform is as the origin and maintainer of those standards. By year three, switching away from FrankMax means switching away from the legibility standard that regulators and auditors expect.
