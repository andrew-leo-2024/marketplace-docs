# Failure Intelligence Feeds

## What It Is

Failure Intelligence Feeds is the byproduct of logging, classifying, and analyzing every AI failure, error, exception, near-miss, and unexpected behavior across the entire platform. When an AI model hallucinates, a workflow produces an incorrect output, a compliance check generates a false positive, or an automated decision is overridden by a human, the platform captures the full context: what failed, why, under what conditions, and how it was resolved.

This creates the most comprehensive AI failure library in existence. Individual organizations experience AI failures sporadically and lack the sample size to identify patterns. The platform sees failures across thousands of organizations, millions of AI actions, and hundreds of use cases simultaneously. It can identify: "GPT-4 hallucination rates spike 3x when processing legal text with nested conditional clauses" or "compliance classification accuracy drops 15% when processing documents from jurisdictions not in the training data." This cross-customer failure intelligence is the single most valuable Kitchen byproduct because it is the foundation for all risk pricing, insurance underwriting, and governance design.

## How It's Generated

Every AI operation on the platform generates failure intelligence data:

- Failure events: full context capture including inputs, model state, configuration, and output
- Classification: failure type taxonomy (hallucination, drift, bias, edge case, integration error, etc.)
- Root cause analysis: automated and human-confirmed root cause attribution
- Resolution data: how failures were detected, escalated, and resolved
- Recurrence patterns: which failure types recur and under what conditions
- Cross-correlation: which failures in one area predict failures in another

- Insurance companies needing actuarial data for AI risk underwriting

- Regulators developing AI safety standards based on empirical failure data
- Safety researchers studying AI failure modes at production scale
- Legal teams preparing litigation or defense in AI failure cases

## Compounding Effect

Failure Intelligence compounds because each failure teaches the platform something new, and that knowledge prevents future failures for all customers. At 1,000 documented failures, the platform identifies common failure modes. At 10,000 failures, it can predict which deployments are likely to fail based on configuration similarity to past failures. At 100,000 failures, it has a comprehensive failure taxonomy that enables preventive governance -- stopping failures before they occur. This preventive capability is the ultimate moat: the platform that has seen the most failures is the platform best equipped to prevent them.
