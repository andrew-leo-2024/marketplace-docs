## Why Margins Improve Over Time

Burger margins improve because the [Kitchen systems](/economic-model/burger-fries-kitchen#the-kitchen-compounding-moat) compound:

| Kitchen System | Y1 Impact | Y3 Impact | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Model Routing Intelligence** | Routes 60% of requests optimally | Routes 90%+ optimally | 2 years of task-model performance data |
| **Semantic Caching** | 25% cache hit rate | 50%+ cache hit rate | Cache grows with usage volume and domain coverage |
| **Fine-Tuned Models** | 2 industry verticals | 10+ verticals | Each vertical's data trains the next vertical faster |
| **Failure Pattern Library** | Catches 30% of known failure modes | Catches 80%+ | Failure library compounds monotonically |

Fries margins improve because attachment rates increase with habit formation and switching costs:

| Year | Attachment Rate | Driver |
|---|---|---|
| Y1: 35% | Customers buy governance/compliance because regulators require it |
| Y2: 45% | Habit engineering kicks in; customers depend on audit trails and workflow templates |
| Y3: 55% | Full switching cost lock-in; leaving means rebuilding compliance, retraining staff, losing audit history |

What happens when things go wrong:

| Scenario | Attachment Rate | Burger Margin | Y3 Revenue | Y3 Gross Profit | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## The 40% Attachment Rate Threshold

This is the single most important number in the FrankMax economic model.

### Why 40%

At 40% average fries attachment:
- Fries revenue covers the burger subsidy
- Kitchen development is self-funded from operating cash flow
- Customer acquisition is funded from fries margin, not external capital
- The business is **self-sustaining** without additional fundraising

Below 40%:
- Burger subsidy exceeds fries margin
- Kitchen development must be funded externally
- Customer acquisition becomes a cash burn
- The business is a **discount AI reseller** losing money on every transaction

### What Drives Attachment Above 40%

| Driver | Mechanism | Target Attachment Lift |
|---|---|---|
| **Regulatory Requirements** | Governance, audit trails, and compliance wrappers are mandatory for regulated industries | +15-20% baseline |
| **Habit Engineering** | Daily/hourly touchpoints create behavioral dependency ([see strategy](/economic-model/habit-engineering)) | +10-15% over 6 months |
| **Switching Costs** | Audit history, trained models, and workflow templates cannot be exported | +5-10% retention lift |
| **Bundle Pricing** | Fries are cheaper when bought with the burger than standalone | +5-8% at point of sale |
| **Outcome Demonstration** | Show measurable ROI from each fries layer within 30 days | +3-5% conversion lift |

### Monitoring the Threshold

Attachment rate is tracked at three levels:

| Level | Metric | Frequency | Alert Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Portfolio** | Average attachment rate across all customers | Daily | Below 40% for 7 consecutive days |
| **Cohort** | Attachment rate by customer signup month | Weekly | Any cohort below 35% at month 3 |
| **Individual** | Per-customer attachment rate | Real-time | Customer below 25% at month 2 (churn risk) |

## Key Assumptions

| Assumption | Value | Risk If Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| Average customer monthly AI spend | $2K-$5K growing | If customers spend less, need more customers for same revenue |
| Fries attachment rate | 35% Y1 to 55% Y3 | Below 40% sustained = existential (see above) |
| Burger cost reduction from Kitchen | 5% Y1 to 15% Y3 | If caching/routing underperforms, burger margin goes negative |
| Customer churn (annual) | 15% Y1, 10% Y2, 7% Y3 | Higher churn means higher CAC and lower LTV |
| Average fries revenue per customer | $1.4K-$5.5K/month | If customers buy fewer/cheaper fries, margins compress |
| Regulatory tailwind | Moderate (2-3 jurisdictions with AI governance mandates by Y2) | If regulation is delayed, attachment rates for compliance layers drop |
