# Organizational Formalization Catalog

340 governance topologies. 10 control variables. One configurable institutional kernel.

Doc 40 cataloged 340 distinct organizational formalization models drawn from enterprises, sovereign institutions, financial infrastructure, military doctrine, and open-source ecosystems. The exercise proved empirically that the institutional design space is **finite and closed** -- every model is a permutation of the same 10 structural levers.

That finding is the foundation of a product: **organizational firmware** -- machine-readable governance configurations that enterprises install, depend on, and cannot easily remove.

---

## The Core Thesis

Enterprises do not choose "frameworks." They choose constraint architectures that determine:

| Decision | What It Controls |
|---|---|
| Where authority concentrates | Who can veto, who can act, who escalates |
| How capital flows | Buffers, reallocation speed, investment gating |
| How risk is absorbed | Centralized vs dispersed, layered vs flat |
| How decisions escalate | Latency, threshold triggers, override rights |
| How failure is contained | Ring-fencing, waterfall loss absorption, sandboxing |

Most enterprises unknowingly run **3-6 incompatible governance logics simultaneously**. The danger is not choosing the wrong model. It is running contradictory models at the same time:

- Mission Command + Heavy Stage-Gate = paralysis
- Freedom & Responsibility + Strict Risk-Weighted Governance = internal contradiction
- Holacracy + PMO Gatekeeping = structural schizophrenia

The FrankMax Organizational Formalization Catalog surfaces these incompatibilities during onboarding. That is not a catalog. That is **diagnostic clarity**.

## Why This Creates Lock-In

Organizational firmware is hard to rip out for three structural reasons:

### 1. Topology Dependency

Once an enterprise configures its AI agent topology, reporting cadence, escalation paths, and dashboard views around a governance model, switching requires rewiring every automated workflow. Estimated switching cost: **$200K-$2M** per enterprise depending on scale.

### 2. Historical Decision Lineage

The platform preserves traceability and historical decision lineage across every governance configuration change. This audit trail becomes a compliance asset that enterprises cannot recreate on a competitor platform. In regulated industries (banking, defense, healthcare), this lineage is required by law.

### 3. Telemetry Compounding

Every governance configuration generates telemetry: which escalation thresholds fire, which authority distributions produce bottlenecks, which capital buffer levels correlate with failure. This data compounds daily across the installed base and feeds the inference engine. A competitor starting from zero has no training data.

### Switching Cost Engineering

| Lock-In Mechanism | Time to Build | Time to Replace |
|---|---|---|
| Agent topology configuration | 2-4 weeks | 3-6 months |
| Historical decision lineage | Accumulates daily | Irreplaceable |
| Compliance audit trail | Accumulates daily | 12-18 months to rebuild |
| Telemetry-trained inference engine | 6-12 months | 24-36 months |
| Custom governance templates | 1-2 months per template | 3-6 months per template |

Once embedded, organizational firmware becomes the load-bearing wall of enterprise operations. Removing it requires a full structural renovation.
