# Tier 2: High-Value Growth -- Operational Efficiency (Chokepoints 26--50)

Addressable pain with clear ROI but longer sales cycles. These chokepoints represent operational inefficiencies that cost organizations significant money but lack the regulatory urgency of Tier 1. Sales require ROI demonstration rather than compliance fear.

## Strategic Analysis

### ROI-Driven Sales
Unlike Tier 1 (compliance-driven), Tier 2 chokepoints require demonstrable ROI. The sales motion shifts from "you must fix this" to "fixing this saves you $X per month." This makes pilot programs and proof-of-concept deployments critical.

### Horizontal Opportunities
Chokepoints 26, 35, 38, 45, and 49 apply across "All" NAICS sectors. These represent platform-level tools that serve every industry vertical.

### Manufacturing & Construction Concentration
Chokepoints 28, 29, 32, 41, 47, and 50 cluster in manufacturing (311-339) and construction (236-238). These sectors represent high-value wedge opportunities where multiple tools can be bundled into a single entry package.

### Knowledge Continuity
Chokepoint 45 (key-person dependency) is a direct feeder into the Frankmax Institutional Memory service. When the marketplace tool detects the risk, Frankmax services provide the structural remediation.

### Sales Cycle Expectations
Tier 2 tools typically require 60--120 day sales cycles with POC phases. Budget authority sits with VPs and directors (not C-suite). Success metrics are operational (time saved, errors reduced, throughput increased) rather than existential (fines avoided, compliance achieved).
