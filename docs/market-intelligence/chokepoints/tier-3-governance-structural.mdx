## Strategic Analysis

### The Invisible Problem
Tier 3 chokepoints share a common characteristic: they are invisible until they cause a failure. No one budgets for "unclear authority boundaries" until a regulatory investigation reveals that no one authorized a specific AI deployment. No one prioritizes "undocumented exception handling" until an exception crashes a production system.

This makes Tier 3 the hardest to sell directly -- but the most valuable to surface during PIAR and Failure Propagation engagements.

### Governance Gap Categories

**Authority Gaps** (51, 56, 58, 66): No one knows who decides, who owns, or who escalates. These gaps create accountability vacuums that regulators exploit during investigations.

**Documentation Gaps** (53, 57, 60, 64, 71): Decisions happen without records. When records exist, they are inconsistent, outdated, or in email inboxes that no one can search.

**Alignment Gaps** (55, 62, 63): Departments optimize for conflicting metrics. Individuals are incentivized to do things that harm the organization. Staff make commitments that erode margins.

**Enforcement Gaps** (59, 61, 70, 73): Policies exist but are not enforced. Procedures are documented but not followed. Compliance is treated as optional. These gaps create the illusion of governance without the substance.

**Visibility Gaps** (52, 65, 68, 69, 72): Shadow systems, untracked overrides, ungoverned data, and shadow vendor relationships operate outside organizational visibility. What you cannot see, you cannot govern.

### Sales Motion
Tier 3 tools are sold as discovery outputs, not standalone products. The typical path:

1. PIAR or Failure Propagation engagement reveals Tier 3 gaps
2. Gap quantification creates urgency (e.g., "you have 47 email-based approvals per month with no audit trail")
3. Marketplace tool deployed to close the specific gap
4. Tool telemetry feeds back into ongoing Frankmax advisory
