## Entry Product Bundle

The banking wedge enters with three tools that address the most expensive compliance and risk chokepoints:

| Tool | Chokepoint Addressed | Typical Monthly Cost of Inaction |
|---|---|---|
| **AML/KYC Automation Platform** | KYC/AML compliance gaps (Chokepoint #17) | $100K--$10M (fines) |
| **Regulatory Reporting Automator** | Manual/inconsistent regulatory reporting (Chokepoint #10) | $25K--$250K |
| **Real-Time Fraud Detection Engine** | Fraud detection delays (Chokepoint #25) | $50K--$1M |

Combined, these three tools address $175K--$11.25M in monthly cost-of-inaction. At an $85K entry price, the ROI is immediate for any institution with material compliance exposure.

---

## Why Banking in Phase 3

Banking enters in Phase 3 alongside professional services:

1. **Regulatory compulsion** -- Banks do not choose to buy compliance tools. Regulators require them. This creates non-optional purchasing behavior with budget authority that does not need ROI justification.
2. **Fine avoidance math** -- A single AML fine can exceed $100M. An $85K tool that reduces fine probability by even 1% has infinite ROI.
3. **Insurance connection** -- Banking (522) and Insurance (524) share compliance infrastructure. Proving the tools in banking creates immediate cross-sell into insurance (the Phase 1 wedge).
4. **Data gravity** -- Banking transaction data is the richest signal source for fraud detection. Every transaction processed through the marketplace tools feeds the failure intelligence library.

## Sales Process

### Week 1--3: Discovery & Qualification
- Review recent regulatory examination findings and remediation commitments
- Quantify false positive rates in existing AML/KYC systems (industry average: 95%+ false positive rate)
- Map regulatory reporting burden: number of reports, frequency, manual effort, error rates

### Week 4--6: Pilot Deployment
- Deploy AML/KYC Automation on a defined customer segment
- Connect Regulatory Reporting Automator to one reporting obligation
- Integrate Fraud Detection with transaction monitoring systems

### Week 7--9: ROI Measurement
- Measure false positive reduction (target: 50%+ reduction)
- Track regulatory reporting accuracy improvement and time savings
- Quantify fraud detection speed improvement (time-to-detection)
- Produce compliance-ready ROI report suitable for board and regulator review

### Week 10--12: Expansion Proposal
- Propose institution-wide deployment across all compliance domains
- Bundle additional tools (Financial Reconciliation Engine, Onboarding Accelerator)
- Structure annual contract with governance layer attachment (critical for banking -- the "fries" sell themselves in regulated environments)
