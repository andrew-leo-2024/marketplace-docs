## D. AINEFF DEPLOYMENT ARCHITECTURE

### ORF/ETLB/MCO Structural Constraints
- **ORF**: Every maintenance deferral decision carries an Obligation Record. When a plant manager defers scheduled maintenance, the ORF documents: the maintenance item, the risk assessment, the projected failure probability, the cost of deferral vs. cost of action, and the name of the human who decided. No anonymous risk accumulation.
- **ETLB**: At the moment any system overrides a safety protocol, quality gate, or compliance requirement, a named human is bound. The operator who bypasses a quality check owns the liability for downstream defects. The plant manager who authorizes production on degraded equipment is bound to the risk.
- **MCO**: Every legacy system integration, every process workaround, every temporary fix carries a mortality date. Maximum lifespan for a "temporary" workaround: 6 months. The Mainframe-to-Cloud Bridge components carry 18-month MCOs. Systems that are not migrated or re-authorized die.

### E-AEGL Governance Hardening
- **Sub-10ms Policy Enforcement**: Every equipment operating parameter monitored against safety and quality thresholds. Violations trigger immediate alerts, not shift-end reports. FDA/ISO/EPA compliance checks automated at the point of operation.
- **SHA-256 Audit Chain**: Every quality measurement, every maintenance event, every compliance document generation is hash-chained. Regulatory submissions carry cryptographic proof of data integrity from sensor to report.
- **Escalation Workflows**: Equipment degradation above threshold escalates from operator to maintenance supervisor (1hr SLA) to plant manager (4hr SLA) to VP Operations (24hr SLA). No bottleneck in the escalation path.

### AI-Native Coordination (43 Core Systems)
- **Predictive Maintenance Platform**: Sensor-driven failure prediction. Replaces reactive break-fix with planned interventions. Targets 90% reduction in unplanned downtime ($260K/hour saved per avoided incident).
- **Process Mining & Optimization Engine**: Discovers actual process flows vs. documented. Identifies the 60% divergence and maps optimization paths.
- **Tribal Knowledge Extractor**: Captures undocumented expertise from retiring workforce through structured interviews, observation analysis, and procedure documentation. Converts 28 years of tribal knowledge into searchable, executable procedures.
- **Quality Prediction Engine**: Predicts defects from upstream process data before they occur. Targets DPMO reduction from 500+ to under 50.
- **Legacy System Migration Planner**: Maps all dependencies in legacy systems. Sequences migration to minimize operational disruption. Identifies critical path and risk concentration.
- **Mainframe-to-Cloud Bridge**: Creates translation layers between COBOL/mainframe systems and modern AI. No rip-and-replace -- incremental modernization.
- **Energy Consumption Optimizer**: Identifies waste patterns across all facilities. Targets 15-25% energy cost reduction.

### Audience-Specific Incentive Alignment
- **Inventory Optimization Engine**: Dynamically optimizes inventory levels. Makes the cost of over-ordering visible to the same managers who authorize purchases.
- **Supplier Dependency Risk Scorer**: Makes single-source dependency quantifiable. Ties procurement performance to supply chain resilience metrics, not lowest-unit-cost alone.
- **Compliance Documentation Generator**: Generates regulatory documentation from process data, not manual compilation. Redirects 2,000+ person-hours from documentation to operations.

### Information Integrity Systems
- **Enterprise Knowledge Graph** (adapted for manufacturing): Maps all critical process knowledge. Identifies knowledge concentration risk. Prioritizes extraction from highest-risk knowledge holders.
- **Quality Prediction Engine**: Converts sensor data into actionable quality intelligence before defects escape production.

## E. ACCOUNTABILITY DESIGN

| Mechanism | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Single-point accountability (ETLB)** | Every maintenance deferral, quality override, and safety bypass binds to a named human at the moment of action. Plant Manager who defers maintenance on Line 7 is bound to that risk until maintenance is completed. Shift supervisor who overrides a quality gate owns the liability for that batch. |
| **Decision rights (ORF)** | Maintenance scheduling authority encoded as ORF constraints. Production cannot override scheduled maintenance without documented risk acceptance and escalation. Procurement cannot sole-source above $50K without documented supplier risk assessment. Quality gates cannot be bypassed without ORF-recorded rationale and MCO-enforced review date. |
| **Escalation (E-AEGL)** | Tier 1: Equipment sensor anomalies escalate to maintenance within 1 hour. Tier 2: Quality prediction alerts escalate to production supervisor within 30 minutes. Tier 3: Safety threshold breaches trigger immediate production halt and escalation to VP Operations. No override possible without ETLB binding. |
| **Ratification layers (MCO)** | Every temporary process workaround expires in 6 months. Every legacy system integration bridge expires in 18 months. Every deferred maintenance decision expires in 90 days (must be completed or re-deferred with escalation). No zombie workarounds. No perpetual technical debt accumulation without human re-authorization. |
