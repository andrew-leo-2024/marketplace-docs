## D. AINEFF DEPLOYMENT ARCHITECTURE

### ORF/ETLB/MCO Structural Constraints
- **ORF**: Every credit decision, every underwriting approval, every trade execution carries an Obligation Record. The ORF captures: the decision, the model used, the human authorizer, the risk parameters at time of decision, and the conditions under which the decision must be reviewed. No credit approval without documented finality of responsibility.
- **ETLB**: At the moment of loan origination, trade execution, claims settlement, or underwriting bind, the named human (loan officer, trader, claims adjuster, underwriter) is cryptographically bound to the decision. Regulatory examination can trace any decision to a single accountable human within minutes, not months.
- **MCO**: Every risk model carries a mortality date. No credit model survives beyond 24 months without revalidation. No actuarial assumption persists beyond the data window it was calibrated on. Every automated trading strategy carries a kill date. Every AML screening rule set expires and must be re-authorized with evidence of effectiveness.

### E-AEGL Governance Hardening
- **Sub-10ms Policy Enforcement**: Every transaction screened against AML/KYC/sanctions in real-time. Every credit decision validated against risk appetite limits before commitment. Every claims settlement checked against policy terms and fraud indicators before payment.
- **SHA-256 Audit Chain**: Every transaction, every model output, every human override is hash-chained. Regulatory examination receives court-grade evidence. Cross-border regulatory requests answered from a single immutable audit source.
- **SOC 2 Continuous Compliance**: Not annual audits -- continuous verification. Every system interaction logged and verified against compliance policies in real-time.
- **Escalation Workflows**: Fraud detection above $50K escalates to investigation unit with 4hr SLA. AML suspicious activity escalates to compliance officer with 2hr SLA. Model performance degradation escalates to Chief Risk Officer with 24hr SLA. Capital adequacy threshold breach escalates to Board Risk Committee with 1hr SLA.

### AI-Native Coordination (43 Core Systems)
- **Claims Processing Accelerator**: Reduces claims cycle from 30+ days to hours. AI-powered triage, validation, estimation, and settlement. Targets 80% straight-through processing for standard claims.
- **AML/KYC Automation Platform**: Reduces false positive rate from 95%+ to under 25%. Saves $28B+ in wasted industry compliance effort. Real-time entity resolution across data sources.
- **Fraud Detection Neural Network**: Real-time transaction scoring. Detects patterns across transaction networks, not individual transactions. Reduces detection latency from days to milliseconds.
- **Credit Risk Modeler**: Dynamic, explainable credit models. Updates continuously with new data, not annual recalibration. Fair lending compliance baked into model architecture.
- **Underwriting Intelligence Engine**: AI-processed risk assessment and pricing. Consistent underwriting across all channels and geographies.
- **Regulatory Reporting Automator**: Automated data aggregation from 200+ internal systems. Report generation in hours, not weeks. Multi-jurisdiction compliance from single data model.
- **Cyber Insurance Risk Modeler**: Portfolio-level cyber risk modeling. Identifies correlation across policies that traditional actuarial methods miss.
- **Actuarial Model Accelerator**: 10-100x acceleration of actuarial modeling. Enables real-time reserve adjustments and pricing updates.

### Audience-Specific Incentive Alignment
- **Parametric Insurance Designer**: Automated trigger-based payouts. Removes claims processing incentive to delay. Aligns insurer and policyholder interests at the point of design.
- **Trade Surveillance Engine**: Makes market manipulation detection continuous, not sample-based. Removes trader incentive to game surveillance windows.
- **Wealth Management Copilot**: AI-augmented advisory. Makes advisor performance measurable against benchmark. Reduces suitability risk through systematic recommendation validation.

### Information Integrity Systems
- **Policyholder Behavior Predictor**: Multi-dimensional lapse and behavior modeling. Replaces stale actuarial assumptions with real-time behavioral intelligence.

## E. ACCOUNTABILITY DESIGN

| Mechanism | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Single-point accountability (ETLB)** | Every credit decision binds the loan officer. Every underwriting approval binds the underwriter. Every claims settlement binds the adjuster. Every trade binds the trader. Every model deployment binds the model risk officer who approved it. No "the model recommended it" defense. No "the committee approved it" diffusion. |
| **Decision rights (ORF)** | Risk appetite framework encoded as ORF constraints. Concentration limits, counterparty exposure limits, claims authority limits, trading limits -- all computationally enforced. Exceptions require documented rationale, CRO approval, and MCO-enforced review date within 90 days. |
| **Escalation (E-AEGL)** | Tier 1: Transaction-level alerts (fraud, AML, policy violations) -- real-time automated response with 2hr human review SLA. Tier 2: Portfolio-level alerts (concentration, correlation, capital adequacy) -- 4hr escalation to CRO. Tier 3: Systemic alerts (model failure, regulatory enforcement, cyber incident) -- 1hr escalation to CEO and Board Risk Committee. |
| **Ratification layers (MCO)** | Every risk model expires in 24 months. Every automated decision rule expires in 12 months. Every delegated authority (trading limit, claims authority, underwriting authority) expires and requires renewal with performance evidence. Every AML screening rule set expires and must demonstrate effectiveness or is replaced. Dead models and dead rules cannot self-resurrect. |
