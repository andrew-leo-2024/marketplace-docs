## C. 3-5 Year Decay Model

**Financial Cost:**
- Curriculum obsolescence drives enrollment decline of 3-7% annually in affected programs. At $40K average tuition, a 1,000-student program loses $1.2M-$2.8M/year.
- Reproducibility crisis erodes grant success rates from 20% to 12-15% over 5 years. For a $50M research portfolio, this represents $12.5M-$20M in lost funding.
- IP undercommercialization forfeits $2M-$10M annually per major research university in licensing revenue.

**Trust Erosion:**
- Public confidence in higher education has declined from 57% (2015) to 36% (2025). Continued decline to 25% by 2029 triggers political intervention (funding cuts, regulatory mandates).
- Reproducibility crisis undermines research credibility with policymakers and industry partners. Research partnerships decline 15-25% over 5 years.

**Competitive Vulnerability:**
- AI-native education platforms (Coursera, Khan Academy, corporate universities) deliver skills faster at 5-10% of the cost. Traditional institutions lose the skills-delivery market within 5 years.
- Open-source AI models democratize research compute, eliminating the capital advantage of elite institutions. Mid-tier universities that cannot differentiate on governance and coordination become redundant.

**Security Fragility:**
- Research data (bioweapons, AI capabilities, classified work) sits on university networks with consumer-grade security. A single breach at a tier-1 research university exposes 10-50 sensitive projects.
- Foreign influence operations target think tanks through funding, visiting scholar programs, and publication pressure. 30%+ of major think tanks have undisclosed foreign funding dependencies.

## D. AINEFF Deployment Architecture

### ORF/ETLB/MCO Structural Constraints

- **ORF applied to grant obligations:** Every grant creates obligations (deliverables, reporting, data sharing). ORF tracks each obligation from creation through finality. Prevents the current state where 40%+ of grant-funded projects fail to deliver required data sharing commitments with zero accountability.
- **ETLB applied to research outputs:** Every AI-assisted research finding binds a named researcher (PI) at the moment of computation. No anonymous AI-generated results. No "the model found this" without a human signature. Directly addresses reproducibility crisis by creating immutable accountability chains.
- **MCO applied to curricula and AI systems:** Every curriculum module, every AI teaching assistant, every research AI tool has an enforced expiry (max 24 months). No curriculum persists without re-authorization. No AI lab tool runs beyond its validated lifespan. Kills zombie courses and unmaintained research software.

### E-AEGL Governance Hardening

- Sub-10ms policy enforcement on research data access. SHA-256 hash-chained audit logs for all AI-assisted research. No ML in the critical path of research integrity decisions.
- Escalation workflows: AI flags reproducibility concerns -> Lab director review (4h SLA) -> Dean review (24h SLA) -> Provost notification (48h SLA). SOC 2 compliant research data handling.
- Docker-deployable across university IT infrastructure without requiring cloud vendor dependency.

### Incentive Alignment Redesign

- Replace publish-or-perish with impact-verified-output. Research Impact Quantifier (marketplace tool) measures real-world impact beyond citation counts.
- Grant funding tied to ORF obligation fulfillment rates, not just paper counts. Institutions with >85% obligation completion get preferential scoring.
- Adjunct compensation restructured through Talent-to-Task Matching Engine to match pay to outcome quality, not course volume.

### Information Integrity Systems

- Enterprise Knowledge Graph with time-indexed knowledge decay. Every research finding carries a confidence score that mechanically degrades unless revalidated (MCO principle applied to knowledge).
- Regulatory Change Tracker for education regulation (Title IV, accreditation standards, export controls on research).
- Cross-institutional data sharing governed by ORF (obligations are trackable) and ETLB (every data access has a named human responsible).

## E. Accountability Design

| Layer | Implementation |
|---|---|
| **Single-point accountability (ETLB)** | Every AI-assisted research output binds the PI's cryptographic signature at computation time. Every curriculum change binds the department chair. Every grant expenditure binds the PI and the grants office director. |
| **Decision rights (ORF)** | Obligation routing: Grant obligations route to PI (execution) + Dean (oversight) + Provost (escalation). Curriculum obligations route to department chair (execution) + curriculum committee (review) + accreditor (validation). Each obligation has exactly one terminal state: fulfilled, expired, or defaulted. |
| **Escalation (E-AEGL)** | Research integrity concerns: automated detection -> lab director (4h) -> department ethics committee (48h) -> institutional review board (5 business days) -> federal agency notification (if required). Zero discretion in escalation timelines. |
| **Ratification layers (MCO)** | Every AI system used in research or teaching has a maximum 24-month lifespan. Renewal requires ETLB-bound re-authorization from department head + IT security review. No auto-renewals. Expired systems lose all access to research data immediately. |
