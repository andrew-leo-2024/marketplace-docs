## D. AINEFF DEPLOYMENT ARCHITECTURE

### ORF/ETLB/MCO Structural Constraints
- **ORF**: Every standards recommendation, every policy advocacy position, every industry benchmark carries an Obligation Record. When the body publishes an industry standard, the ORF documents: the evidence base, the committee composition, the dissenting views, the applicability scope, and the review schedule. No standard without accountability for its basis.
- **ETLB**: When the body advocates a policy position to a regulator, the Executive Director or Policy Director is bound as the human accountable for the accuracy of the evidence presented. When the body publishes benchmarking data, the research director is bound to the methodology and data integrity.
- **MCO**: Every published standard carries a mortality date (maximum 36 months). Every policy position carries a review date (maximum 12 months). Every benchmarking methodology expires and must be re-validated with current data. No zombie standards persisting decades past relevance. No advocacy positions surviving the regulatory environment they addressed.

### E-AEGL Governance Hardening
- **Sub-10ms Policy Enforcement**: All published data and standards validated against source evidence before distribution. Member data aggregation validated against antitrust compliance boundaries in real-time.
- **SHA-256 Audit Chain**: Every standards committee deliberation, every policy recommendation, every benchmarking data point is hash-chained. Members can verify the evidence trail behind any published position.
- **Escalation Workflows**: Member disengagement patterns detected by Member Engagement Predictor trigger outreach within 48 hours. Regulatory changes with material member impact trigger policy response workflow within 72 hours. Standards committee stalls (>6 months without progress) auto-escalate to Executive Director.

### AI-Native Coordination (43 Core Systems)
- **Industry Benchmarking Engine**: AI-synthesized benchmarking from public data sources. Eliminates dependency on voluntary survey responses. Achieves equivalent data quality from 200+ public data signals vs. 20% survey response rate.
- **Regulatory Impact Modeler**: Quantifies impact of proposed regulations on industry members in dollar terms. Transforms qualitative advocacy into quantified evidence. Regulators receive impact analysis within 72 hours of proposed rule publication.
- **Skills Gap Analyzer**: Maps workforce demand vs. supply by role, region, and skill level across the entire industry. Uses job posting data, credential databases, and employer surveys.
- **Innovation Radar**: Systematic global scanning of technology trends, patents, publications, and startup activity relevant to the industry. Replaces conference-based awareness with continuous intelligence.
- **Industry Standards Compiler**: AI-accelerated standards drafting from best-practice synthesis. Reduces standards development from 5-10 years to 6-12 months.
- **Trade Dispute Intelligence**: Models cascading effects of tariffs and trade disputes on industry members. Quantifies exposure by company size and geography.

### Audience-Specific Incentive Alignment
- **Member Engagement Predictor**: Identifies disengagement patterns before members formally exit. Triggers proactive value demonstration. Makes membership renewal a measured, managed process.
- **Event & Conference Optimizer**: AI-optimized event programming, matchmaking, and sponsorship. Measures ROI per attendee, not just attendance counts. Aligns event design with member value, not sponsor revenue.
- **Supply Chain Sector Monitor**: Monitors systemic supply chain risks across all members. Creates collective intelligence that no individual member could achieve alone. This is the value proposition that justifies membership.

### Information Integrity Systems
- **Collective Bargaining Intelligence**: Data-supported negotiation positions. Models scenarios and precedents for labor negotiations across the industry.
- **Industry Benchmarking Engine**: Replaces survey-dependent benchmarking with AI-synthesized intelligence from public data. Makes benchmarking reliable and continuous.

## E. ACCOUNTABILITY DESIGN

| Mechanism | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Single-point accountability (ETLB)** | Executive Director bound to accuracy of all published standards, benchmarking data, and policy positions. Policy Director bound to regulatory advocacy evidence quality. Research Director bound to benchmarking methodology integrity. Board Chair bound to governance decisions affecting member interests. No "the committee decided" diffusion. |
| **Decision rights (ORF)** | Board decision rights encoded as ORF constraints. Standards publication requires documented evidence base and dissent record. Policy advocacy requires quantified impact analysis. Dues increases require documented value-per-member analysis. Event programming requires projected member ROI analysis. All subject to MCO-enforced review. |
| **Escalation (E-AEGL)** | Tier 1: Member engagement decline detected -- automated outreach within 48 hours. Tier 2: Standards committee stall (>6 months) -- escalation to Executive Director with 7-day resolution SLA. Tier 3: Regulatory change with material member impact -- policy response initiated within 72 hours. Tier 4: Membership decline exceeding 5% annually -- board emergency session within 14 days. |
| **Ratification layers (MCO)** | Every published standard expires in 36 months. Every policy position expires in 12 months. Every benchmarking report expires upon publication of successor. Every board strategic plan expires in 24 months. No zombie publications. No outdated policy positions persisting as the body's stated view. Renewal requires evidence of continued relevance. |
