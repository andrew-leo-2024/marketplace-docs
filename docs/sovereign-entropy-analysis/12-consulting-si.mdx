## A. Entropy Vector Map

| Dimension | Entropy Vector | Specific Manifestation |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategy** | AI commoditizing the core value proposition | Consulting firms sell expertise arbitrage: "We know things you don't." AI collapses expertise scarcity. McKinsey-quality analysis is now available for $20/month via Claude. The strategic value of human consultants is migrating from "knowing" to "doing" and "governing" -- but 80%+ of consulting revenue still comes from the knowing layer. |
| **Operations** | Utilization-driven staffing destroying quality | The billable hour model requires 70-80% utilization to maintain margins. This creates systematic overwork, knowledge-thin delivery, and consultant burnout. Senior partners sell, juniors deliver. The quality gap between what was sold and what is delivered widens annually. SI projects fail at 70%+ rates. |
| **Incentives** | Partner economics misaligned with client outcomes | Partners are compensated on revenue generation, not client outcome quality. This creates a structural incentive to scope larger engagements, extend timelines, and staff with junior resources. The client's incentive (fast, cheap, effective) is orthogonal to the partner's incentive (large, long, highly staffed). |
| **Information** | Knowledge walking out the door with every departure | A consulting firm's intellectual capital exists in the heads of its consultants. Average consultant tenure is 2-4 years. With 25-35% annual turnover, the firm's knowledge base turns over completely every 3-4 years. Deliverable reuse rates are below 20% because knowledge management systems capture documents, not expertise. |
| **Culture** | Up-or-out creating institutional amnesia | The up-or-out model ensures that no one stays in a role long enough to develop deep operational knowledge. Every 18-24 months, the person who understood a client relationship, a methodology, or a domain is replaced by someone who does not. Institutional learning is impossible when the institution continuously expels its learners. |
| **Capital** | Bench costs and proposal investment destroying margins | Bench costs (consultants between engagements) consume 8-15% of revenue. Proposal development consumes 3-7% of revenue with 15-25% win rates. Combined, 11-22% of revenue is consumed before any value is delivered. |
| **Governance** | Engagement scoping failures creating liability exposure | Scope creep is the default state. 60%+ of engagements exceed original scope. Responsibility boundaries between consultant and client are contractually vague. When an SI implementation fails (70% probability), the liability allocation is disputed, unresolvable, and litigated. |

## C. 3-5 Year Decay Model

**Financial Cost:**
- AI-driven commoditization compresses advisory margins from 40-60% to 15-25% over 5 years. A $500M advisory practice loses $125M-$175M in margin.
- SI implementation failure rates (70%) cost the average firm $10M-$50M annually in warranty remediation, litigation, and relationship repair.
- Knowledge attrition forces continuous rehiring and retraining at $50K-$150K per senior consultant replacement. At 30% turnover in a 1,000-person firm, that is $15M-$45M annually.

**Trust Erosion:**
- Clients increasingly view consulting as interchangeable. Brand premium declines 20-30% over 5 years as AI-powered alternatives demonstrate equivalent or superior analytical capability.
- Post-engagement NPS scores decline 5-10 points annually as the gap between partner-sold and junior-delivered widens.

**Competitive Vulnerability:**
- Boutique AI-native consulting firms emerge with 3-5x productivity per consultant. A 20-person AI-augmented firm delivers equivalent output to a 100-person traditional firm.
- AgentCoders-style autonomous development teams eliminate the need for large SI teams. A 6-quality-gate automated pipeline replaces 30-50 manual QA positions per engagement.
- Clients build internal AI capability, reducing outsourcing demand by 20-40% within 3 years.

**Security Fragility:**
- Consulting firms access sensitive client data across hundreds of engagements. A single breach exposes multiple clients simultaneously. Cross-client data contamination (using one client's data to inform another's engagement) is undetectable without audit infrastructure.

## D. AINEFF Deployment Architecture

### ORF/ETLB/MCO Structural Constraints

- **ORF applied to engagement obligations:** Every consulting engagement generates obligations (deliverables, milestones, knowledge transfer). ORF tracks each from creation to finality. Clean separation: which obligations belong to the consulting firm vs. the client vs. shared. When a scope change occurs, new obligations are created and old ones are explicitly expired -- no scope creep by accretion.
- **ETLB applied to advisory outputs:** Every recommendation, every implementation decision, every architecture choice binds a named consultant at execution time. When an SI implementation fails, the ETLB chain shows exactly who made which decisions and whether they had the authority to do so. Eliminates the current ambiguity: "the consulting firm recommended it" is replaced by "Sarah Chen, Principal Architect, authorized this database schema on 2026-04-15 at 14:23 UTC."
- **MCO applied to engagement artifacts and AI tools:** Every deliverable has an enforced validity period (max 12 months for strategic advice, max 6 months for technology recommendations). AI tools used during engagements have maximum 12-month lifespans. No client inherits an unmaintained AI system from an engagement that ended 3 years ago.

### E-AEGL Governance Hardening

- Client data isolation enforced at the infrastructure level. Sub-10ms policy checks on every cross-client data access. SHA-256 hash-chained logs for all engagement data access.
- Escalation workflow for scope changes: Consultant -> Engagement Manager (4h) -> Partner (24h) -> Client steering committee (48h). No unilateral scope expansion.
- SOC 2 compliance for all engagement data handling. Docker-deployable governance layer that travels with the engagement, not the firm's internal IT.

### Incentive Alignment Redesign

- Replace revenue-per-partner with client-outcome-per-partner. ORF obligation fulfillment rates become the primary metric: did the engagement deliver what was promised?
- Knowledge contribution metrics: consultants measured on reusable deliverables created, not just billable hours consumed. Knowledge Reuse Engine tracks attribution.
- Scope discipline: ETLB-bound scope definitions mean partners cannot verbally expand scope without creating auditable obligation records.

### Information Integrity Systems

- Knowledge Reuse Engine with MCO-enforced validity periods. Strategic analyses expire after 12 months. Technology assessments expire after 6 months. Market sizing expires after 9 months.
- Tribal Knowledge Extractor deployed at every separation event. When a consultant departs, their engagement knowledge is systematically captured before their ETLB authorizations expire.
- Client relationship intelligence that survives individual partner departures. Relationship maps are institutional, not personal.

## E. Accountability Design

| Layer | Implementation |
|---|---|
| **Single-point accountability (ETLB)** | Every engagement deliverable binds the delivering consultant + reviewing partner. Every scope change binds the requesting partner + approving client sponsor. Every SI architecture decision binds the architect + the approving technical authority. |
| **Decision rights (ORF)** | Engagement obligations route to delivery lead (execution) + partner (oversight) + client sponsor (acceptance). Finality requires client sign-off -- no self-certifying deliverable completion. Scope change obligations route through explicit ORF creation/expiry cycle. |
| **Escalation (E-AEGL)** | Implementation risk detected: delivery lead (2h) -> engagement partner (8h) -> practice lead (24h) -> managing partner (48h). Client notification mandatory within 72h of material risk detection. No concealment of engagement risk. |
| **Ratification layers (MCO)** | Every engagement AI tool has a max 12-month lifespan tied to the engagement contract. Post-engagement: tools either transfer to client (with new MCO) or terminate. No orphaned AI systems. Every strategic recommendation carries a validity date (MCO-enforced). |
